The Tyranny of Pre-digestion

Le seul véritable voyage, le seul bain de Jouvence, ce ne serait pas d'aller vers de nouveaux paysages, mais d'avoir d'autres yeux, de voir l'univers avec les yeux d'un autre, de cent autres, de voir les cent univers que chacun d'eux voit, que chacun d'eux est; et cela, nous le pouvons avec un Elstir, avec un Vinteuil; avec leurs pareils, nous volons vraiment d'étoiles en étoiles.

—Marcel Proust

Preemptively making a point, this quote is often bastardized as simply: The real voyage of discovery consists not in seeking new landscapes, but in having new eyes.

The modern internet experience is like eating a meal where every bite has been pre-chewed. Before we read an article, we know how many people liked it. Before we watch a film, we've read its Letterboxd reviews. The origin of our opinion begins with others, and as a result, we no longer rely on our own experience as the primacy of our opinion.

What if our next generation of tools said fuck-all to consensus and valued one's own distance?

I've been working on a 2x2 that plots coordination (social + technological) against implementation (individual + collective):

  • Technological Sovereignty: Technological solutions, Individual Implementation
  • Algorithmic Transparency: Technological solutions, Collective Implementation
  • Platform-independent Identity: Social coordination, individual implementation
  • Cooperative Economics: Social coordination, collective implementation

While that 2x2 deserves a piece in its own right, the 4 bullets map nicely onto the field of our networked experience. What might we build?
